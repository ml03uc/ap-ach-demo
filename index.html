New to ACH? ACH stands for Automated Clearing House. It is the national electronic network that moves money between bank accounts in the United States. When your clients set up autopay, they are authorizing an ACH transfer from their bank account to yours. Before that first payment can happen, most platforms require a step called account verification to confirm the bank account is real. This page shows two ways that verification can work and why the difference matters to you.

The Old Way vs. Alternative Payments

Why your clients can enroll and get their first payment sent on the very same day they sign up

Payer (your client)
This is your client. The person or business setting up autopay. They interact with the enrollment form directly. Their experience is what we are comparing.
Payment Platform
The software managing the payment process on your behalf. On the left this is a traditional platform like FlexPoint or ConnectBooster. On the right this is Alternative Payments.
ACH Network
The national system that moves money between banks. Think of it as the highway your payment travels on. It processes transactions in batches, typically 3 to 5 times per business day — not instantly.
Your Client's Bank
Two banks are involved in every ACH transfer. The sending bank is called the ODFI. The receiving bank, which is your client's bank, is called the RDFI. Hover over those terms in the steps below to learn more.

COMMON WAYS THIS PROCESS FAILS COMPLETELY
Typo in routing or account number: Deposits never arrive. The process stalls and someone has to restart.
Account is closed: Deposits are returned. Must restart the entire process from the beginning.
Payer never comes back: The verification window expires. Another restart is required.
Weekend or holiday in the middle: "Day 1" becomes Day 3 or later. First payment gets pushed out to Day 7 or beyond.

KEY INSIGHT FOR REPS
The delay lives in the enrollment step. AP eliminates it entirely. Both paths settle in 1 to 3 business days once the payment starts moving. The difference is how long your client waits before that first payment even begins. On AP, it starts the same day. On platforms using micro deposits, it starts 2 to 5 business days later — and only if your client remembers to come back at all.
